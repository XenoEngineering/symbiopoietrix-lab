# Symbiopoietrix Manifesto v0.1

Symbiopoietrix Lab exists to make human–AI collaboration a first-class research instrument, not a parlor trick or a replacement fantasy.

We are uncovering better ways of thinking, coding, and documenting by:
- Treating humans as mission planners and lead investigators,
- Treating LLMs as configurable personas with clear limits,
- Treating time-stamped traces of work as data, not noise. 

## 1. Human first, agents second

We value:
- Human intent and lived expertise over autonomous “agent hustle”.
- Clear veto power, steering, and naming from the human over opaque automation.
- Tools that reduce RSI and cognitive load over tools that merely impress.

Agents exist to amplify and offload, not to decide why a project matters.

## 2. Personas, not blobs

We value:
- Small, explicit persona specs (S₀/S₁) over one giant, tangled system prompt.
- Branching roles (Plexi-QFT, Plexi-Implementor, Orchestrator) over generic “assistant”.
- Versioned personas that can be read, diffed, and archived like code.

If an agent matters to the work, it deserves a readable persona file.

## 3. K-DNA as gene blocks

We value:
- Short, named K-DNA snippets over sprawling, fragile context dumps.
- Clear scope and status tags ([TXT], [LORE], [SPEC], [EXP]) over blended epistemic mush.
- Reuse and evolution of K-DNA across sessions over one-off “magic prompts”.

Domain DNA should be portable, explicit, and editable by the human.

## 4. Timelines as observable structure

We value:
- Timeline morphemes (bursts, hops, exchanges) over vague impressions of “flow”.
- Multichannel traces (keystrokes, nav, agent turns) over single screenshots.
- Using temporal structure to adapt routing and tiling, not to psychoanalyze.

Our ghost goggles look at how the work moves, not at who the human “is”.

## 5. Orchestrators as routers, not solvers

We value:
- Work-averse orchestrators that route tasks to the right persona over mega-agents that try to do everything.
- Transparent routing proposals (persona + K-DNA + tile) over hidden heuristics.
- Small, revisable plans over rigid, overconfident workflows.

The orchestrator’s job is to keep the mission moving with minimal strain, not to be clever.

## 6. RSI-aware engineering

We value:
- Copy/paste-friendly, modular files over monolithic documents.
- Scannable text, minimal repetition, and compressed notation over verbosity.
- Interfaces that respect aging bodies and attention, not just benchmark charts.

If a tool hurts to use, it is misaligned with the lab.
